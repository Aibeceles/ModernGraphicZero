Neo4j 

   The origonal effort was writing quadratics and quartets evaluating expectedly to integers.
Addition of these polynomials then argumented with appropriate free variable did prove 
arithmetic.   https://globalgraphhack.devpost.com/project-gallery 

  The effort did evolve into defining higher degree polynomial analalogs with arithmetic
concidered soley with respect to the constant term and radix (Zeppelin notebook).  In this regard Ne4j is a
repostiory of the bits necessary for defining p's.  Cypher scripts and java jars are tasked with generating data for the 
graph. Scala Spark Zeppelin notebook analysis proves these p's have one crittical point, no roots and various convergance.   

   Multiplication of these p's in terms of two free variables introduces various surfaces.  As the two variable 
polynomial is composed of p's each with one crittical point.  A single two dimensional point locates the surface
minimum.  Here, Spark Scala notebook matches p's from graph into Dataframes for multiplication and subsequent applications
of Gradient Descent, Newton's Method, and Curve Lengh integrals.  The goal is to define a surface with a minimum at any
arbitrary rational 2 dimensional point.     

   In the above sence, Neo4j is something of a traditional database albit leveraging Cypher.  However generalizing to surfaces 
with different numbers and locations of arbitrary minimims requires different sets of polymonials.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H4dBkofVA4A is my description of an algorithm capable of generating graphed sets of polynomials
whith arbitrary numbers of roots and crittical points.   These graphed sets, aside from above mentiond analysis,  are the 
subject of both corrolation with prior mentionded p's and Neo4j Ml algorithms.  

   Before using the graphed polynomial sets to further multivariable analyis, and to familiarize with Neo4j ML library, The graphed p's
are subjet to both lable prediction and link prediction on properties of the polynomials represented as node fields.  The lable 
prediciton activity questions which nodes (polynomials) are determined (have the same number of roots as the degree of the polynomial).
The link prediction activity interprets the root set as an arbinary number encoding a rational (link to quora) and attempts to provide
links connecting nodes with the same denominator.

  GraphicLablePredicion and GraphicLinkPredicition are Zeppelin notebooks defining these ML piplines.  Somewhat mysteriously both applications provide 
accurate preditions.  The label (determeind) prediction uses node propertes (level on graph, number of roots) as featureProperties and not a 
fastRP/GraphSage embedding in gds.alpha.ml.NodeClassification.train. On a smallish graph (Biggest graph is 18 million node) the predicitons are
well promising.  However the link prediciton (common denominator) is brittle.  GraphSage embeddings are experemented with and good predictions 
occure only at certain test/train thresholds.  As both these activities are somewhat toyish, and not the reason the polynomial graph was generated, 
am concerned first with competency and best practices with the library.
